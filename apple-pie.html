How to make Apple Pie



Ingredients

Two 9" pie crusts, one for the bottom and one for the top (homemade or store bought, either will be okay!)
7 large apples (peeled, cored, and sliced)
½ cup granulated sugar
½ cup brown sugar
2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
⅛ teaspoon ground nutmeg
1 tablespoon lemon juice (plus the zest of half of a lemon)
1 large egg (lightly beat since this will be used as an egg wash)
Optional: 2 tablespoons sanding sugar

Instructions

The pie crust should be chilled for an hour before rolling out. One will be used for the bottom while the other will be for the top.
Preheat the oven to 400°F, making sure the oven rack is in the center
Mix in a bowl: apples, granulated sugar, light brown sugar, flour, cinnamon, nutmeg, lemon juice, lemon zest; make sure to coat the apple slices evenly
Remove dough from fridge and let it rest in room temperature for 5-10 minutes before rolling both into a 12" circle that is ⅛" thick. Afterwards, lay the first disc carefully on the bottom of the deep pie plate.
Spoon the apple filling (without the juices) over the bottom crust and lay the second disc over it.
Trim the excess dough and gently press the edges where the two pie crusts meet. Cut 4 slits on the top to allow steam to vent. You can also look for different pie designs to make it more unique to you!
Brush the surface with egg wash and sprinkle with sanding sugar. Use a piece of foil to keep the edges from burning
Bake at 400°F for 25 minutes, then remove the foil, lower the temp to 375°, and continue baking until golden bubbly. Cool at room temperature for 3 hours and enjoy!
